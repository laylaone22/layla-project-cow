MIDDLE OR LOIN CUTS

It is difficult to find enough adjectives to describe the superb cuts that come from the beef loin. Whether bone-in or boneless, all are tender and flavorsome.

RIB EYE STEAKCuts from the forerib, this well-marbled steak is deservedly popular. Top cap or dwckle, a thin muscle lying over the foewrib is sometimes sold separatly for quick-frying.

FILET OR FILLET MIGNON Leaner then loin, this is the most tender cut. When fully trimmed, it makes a luxurious roast, although some prefer more marbled beef. It is also sliced into steaks.

STANDING RIB ROAST, ON THE BONE A superb bone-in rosting cut, this is similar to sirloin, but without the fillet and with more muscle and fat on the outside. It should be well marbled.

ADDITIONAL MIDDLE OR LOIN

•SIRLOIN ROAST, ON THE BONEsimilar to the standing rib roast but also includes the fillet muscle. T-bone steaks are slices of this cut.

•BONELESS RIB EYE ROASRA well-marbled tender roast that is easy to carve.

•SIRLION STEAKA slice of a deboned sieloin, with marbeling and outer fat, a premium steak.

•STRIP STEAKA lean steak from the eze muscke but with all the outer fat removed.

•PORTERHOUSE STEAKCut from the rump end of the sirloin, this is a thick-cut bone-in steak.
